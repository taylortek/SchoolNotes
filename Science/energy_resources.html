Traditional Energy Sources

Renewable Vs. Nonrenewable

  • Renewable can be replenished over a relatively short period of time
    • Examples: Solar, Wood, Geothermal
  • Nonrenewable sources can take millions of years to form and accumulate
    • Examples: Fossil fuels (oil, coal, natural gas), Nuclear

Impacts of Mining Coal

  • Subsurface Mining
    • Fire, collapse, explosion
    • Black lung disease
    • Land subsidence (like a sinkhole)
    • Acid mine drainage into surface and groundwater
  • Surface Mining (open-pit or strip mining)
    • Destruction of ecosystem

Impacts of Oil Use

  • Releases CO2, NOx, SOx, and CO
  • Oil spills
  • Distruption of wildlife habitat
  • (Oil provides the majority of US energy)

Natural Gas Facts

  • Cleanest burning of fossil fuels
  • Considerable amount in the US
  • Produces CO2 (greenhouse gas)
  • Can cause grounwater pollution from extraction (fracking)

Fossil Fuel Alternative

  • Tar Sands
    • Made of clay, water, sand, and bitumen
